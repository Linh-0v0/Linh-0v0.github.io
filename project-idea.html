Public Urination - Littering

Outcome

As a Vietnamese citizen, I noticed some of the problems that is rather delicate in Vietnam that people ignore as it is a very tactful subject: “Public urination”. Along with this, there is also another ubiquitous problem that catches my attention which is “Littering”. In terms of the reasons why these problems take place, as for Public urination, it is awkward for people to ask for a toilet in a convenient store or restaurant. Regarding littering, I figure out that there is a small number of trash bins in public as well as no designated area for litter, thus due to this cause, people have no other choice but to put their household waste at the sidewalk’s sewer or under the tree’s foot which ruins the urban beauty.

Motivation:

Phuong Luong, a VnExpress reader, said:

“Public urination is nothing strange in Vietnam, where there is an acute shortage of public toilets. ‘Frankly, I don’t want to pee in public but I still have to as I can’t find any public toilets, but I also want to be a civilized citizen.’ ”

Source: Link

This news is published in 2016. At that time, it is hard to address this problem due to the shortage of public toilets and technology. Fast forward to 2020, over a period of time, the government has paid attention to this problem due to a considerable increase in the number of foreign visitors, and then have erected many public toilets in the city. On top of that, thanks to the development of e-commerce like Shopee, NowShip, Tiki, Grab, etc. there is a great surge in the number of people using the smartphone. Therefore, I feel that now is a suitable time to have an application helping people locate the closest public toilet and designated dump location.

Description:

From the perspective of the users, I believe that the app needs to have the following features and characteristics:

  1. The focus on UI design to let the users experience comfortable use.
    2. A unique UI design will differentiate this app from the competitors and leaves an impression in the users. The users only need to tap the button to find the toilet or dump areas. Unlike Google, the users don’t need to type because this app emphasizes on locating public WC and designated dump area.
  2. Locate the closest public toilet / trash bins / designated dump areas:
    3. Guide people to the wanted location like Google Maps.
  3. The app can operate domestically and internationally
    4. The users can also use this app to find public toilets and dump locations in foreign countries.
  4. The users can leave reviews about the toilets:
    5. For example, to tell others that this public toilet/designated dump area is cleaned or report something is broken down.

Some of the drawbacks/problems::

  1. There isn’t any designated area for household waste and the government hasn’t known what to do with this problem yet. People in Vietnam do not use outdoor large trash bin like other developed countries, thus they put household waste under tree’s foot or at the side of their house.
  2. To use the public toilet, people must pay a small amount of money (2000-4000 VND / entry). I understand that there must be a small fee to maintain the public toilet. Nevertheless, Vietnamese people are not fond of the fact that they have to pay money to use the public toilet. It will be better if these fees are included in the form of taxes.
  3. The toilet’s hygiene is poor even though people have paid money to use.
  4. Even though there have been more public toilets, it will be even more convenient if the government cooperates with the convenient stores and make the stores’ toilet become a public utility. In this case, to use these toilets, a small fee is applied. The closer the public toilet is, the more likely people will not urinate in public.

Tools and Technologies:

Since locating and tracking technology is advanced and complex, the technology is not open-source software. Some of the technologies may be used:

  • GPS
  • Aggregate location data
  • Local governmental data
  • Real-time feedback from users
  • Third-party map partners (if necessary)

This app has a similar function to Google Map, but it focuses on public utilities, thus the users will only need to tap the button to find the toilet or dump areas. There is no need to type anything, your location will be automatically located so that the app will guide you to the nearest public utility.

Outcome:

If this project succeeds, it is clear that the number of public urination cases will decrease. Littering will occur less as there are trash bins and designated areas for litter. As long as the public toilets and dump areas are at the users’ disposal, the higher chance of the project will be successful. The app only aims to ease the process of finding public utilities. The core of problems that are listed in

“Some of the drawbacks/problems”

still need to be tackled for this app to be successful. The impact I wish the app to have on society is that the urban streets will be clean from trash and there will be no more unpleasant scenes of people urinating at the side of the streets.
