Difference between Cybersecurity and Privacy:

The common purpose of computer protection and privacy is to secure personal data, but they have very different methods. Data security focuses on safeguarding information from fraud and breaches. Whereas secrecy regulates how data is gathered, distributed and used .

WHAT IS THE STATE OF THE ART OF THIS NEW TECHNOLOGY?

The newest defenses in cybersecurity: Source Link

Moving target security
In order to make it harder for ransomware to infect a computer, this type of security scrambles the names, locations and references of files in the server's memory as well as the application itself .
Air gapping
Air gapping is the practice of isolating a device from the local networks and the internet so that the only way to hack the device is to have physical access to the server. This is one of the most secure options for the preservation of sensitive data that must be stored in an ultra-secure system, such as those used for strategic defense or the operation of nuclear generators .
Co-operative cybersecurity
This is also called data-sharing alliance. It means that to steal a data, all the systems must be hacked. Without capability to do this, the hackers cannot get the full files either .
Next-gen firewalls
It is nothing new, however, the next generation firewalls take server protection to the next level. It prevents the websites and mobile applications from viruses, ransomware and malware as well as stopping intruder penetration and stopping attacks from distributed denial of service (DDoS).

  3. Automated and Adaptive Networks
    Adaptive Networks are automated and programmable networks that can customize, track, control and respond to evolving needs. Three specific layers are used for these networks: 1. programmable infrastructure, 2. analytics and intelligence, and 3. software for automatic control.
    The programmable infrastructure layer functions as a sensor and provides real-time data on network output and vulnerabilities, helping agencies to proactively repair them and distribute resources appropriately.
    The end layer is technology for monitoring and automation. It optimizes software-defined network architectures and multi-domain service orchestration to simplify network management and service delivery through multi-vendor, multi-domain hybrid networks. Such layers combine to build a more versatile, elastic and secure network.
    An Adaptive Network lets agencies address rising bandwidth constraints, as well as modernization and security requirements, providing customers with state of the art and faster services.
    Automation enables scanning and tracking of linked networks to report on irregularities and abnormalities in real-time. AI and ML will be one of these networks' main elements and support applications.
  4. Blockchain Cybersecurity
    To hold future cyber-attacks at bay, the use of Blockchain technologies with AI will set up a secure verification system. Why Blockchain Cybersecurity is able to do so?
    - Blockchain is decentralized
    The blockchain divides everything into little bits and delivers them across the entire computing network. It is a digital transaction ledger that doesn’t need a central control point. There is a full ledger’s copy on every computer, or node, so one or two nodes going down would not lead to any loss of data.
    - Everything in blockchain is in encryption.
    Thanks to this nature, you can validate the data has not been changed by checking the file signatures across all the ledges on all nodes
    - Blockchain is impossible to hack virtually.
    Although hackers can break into conventional networks, find all the information in a single archive and steal or corrupt it, Blockchain makes this work unfeasible as the data is encrypted, decentralized, and being cross-checked by the whole network.

    The zero-trust security model is the idea of sustaining the restrictions of access and not trusting anyone by default, even no exception for those who are already inside the network enforced and no one even those already within the network perimeter, is trusted by default. The aim is to use authentication to re-ensure protection in the course of using, but in a careful and restricted manner, to avoid burdening the user.

II. Location-based service (LBS) :

Source: Link

This term refers to technologies that use real-time location tracking to function. It means that the technology persistently recognizes the physical and geographical presence of the device. The technology is most widely used for mobile devices, but it can be extended to any device, even desktop PCs, that can provide a position.

How can it locate your position?

Inside a standard smartphone, there are many mechanisms which can provide information about locations. GPS, RFID, Wi Fi and mobile phones are the most prevalent.
To give you the exact location, a mobile device connects with other devices and hubs such as satellites, routers, towers or something else. Since the mobile device is linked to multiple communication hubs, it can triangulate its exact location.
The definition of the basic technical term that you need to know to better understand location-based service:

Triangulation definition:

In a method called trilateration, a global positioning system (GPS) device uses data from satellites to identify a particular location on the Planet. To trilaterate, a GPS receiver uses radio signals to determine distances from satellites. Trilateration, as you can see in the image, is identical to triangulation, which calculates angles .

Several examples of technologies used to track location. These technologies can be combined to provide the most accurate location.

GPS
GPS is the acronym for “Global Positioning System”, which are satellites revolving around the earth to help locate stuff in the world. Any device with a GPS receiver can receive the GPS signal. The mobile device will be connected to at least four satellites, then satellites will compare the latency of the signal to determine when the signal came from. This makes it easy for your phone to know exactly where you are and to show turn-by-turn navigation.
Wi Fi
For this method of position tracking, IP addresses are used. Every network has a physical IP address that helps the Internet to locate where it is. This is essential in order to transmit data correctly through the Internet infrastructure. When the mobile connects to a Wi-Fi network, it is connected to the network's
Cellular
Cellular tracking operates pretty much like GPS. However, instead of linking to satellites, the machine connects to cellular towers. To put it simply, you are going to be in the vicinity of at least two towers, and that's enough for the system to locate your spot using triangulation.
QR Codes
QR tracking is similar to Wi-Fi. It logs information relevant to the search whenever a dynamic QR code is scanned. When the QR code is identified, its physical location will be registered.
RFID
RFID is an acronym for “radio-frequency identification” .
RFID tracking is essentially the mix of these methods. Generally, the RFID scanner has a static location. When the RFID scanner is enabled, it tags its location and the access. This can be used to recognize where the device accessing the RFID is located.

Practical uses of Location-based services:

  • Locating Store: Retail consumers can easily locate the closest shop location using location-based intelligence.
  • Travelling: An LBS can send information to the device in real-time, like traffic information or weather forecasts, so that the user can prepare accordingly.
  • Marketing: Local advertisers are able to push advertisements to people within the same geographical area. Location-based mobile marketing offers advertisement within the city to targeted clients.
  • Fraud detection: By matching a customer's location through the mobile to a credit card purchase, an LBS provides another level of authentication. Attaching the location of the smartphone to a credit card enables you to mark transactions made through several geographic locations over a limited time span.
